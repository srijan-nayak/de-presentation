Natural-Born Entrepreneur

A presentation on Dan Bricklin's article by group-2

Can you become an entrepreneur?

Can you become an entrepreneur?

  • Dan Bricklin & Bob Frankston created VisiCalc in 1979
  • Case studies in Harvard Business School
  • Manually recalculating a spreadsheet during was time consuming
  • Dan Bricklin: Father of the electronic spreadsheet
  • Your idea may be the next big thing
  • It won't be easy
  • Unless you are passionate about your craft, the risks may outweigh the rewards
  • You need to learn from the lessons of the past

What will people pay for?

  • Don't spend too much time on what might be needed in the far future
  • Spend time considering what it is that people actually want in the present

What might make someone an entrepreneur?

  • Education for technical skills development
  • Business studies
  • Individual responsibility of how to participate
  • Looking at problems from various perspectives
  • Leadership skills

So, are entrepreneurs born or made?

Learning on the fly

  • In a small venture, you need to learn things step-by-step as you go
  • If you fail, try a different route

Some conventional wisdom

  • Hire the right people: Choose people who are flexible
  • Do what you love
  • Don't do it for the dream of money
  • People matter, and focus is important

Appreciate the value others provide

  • Understand your true talent and don't overestimate your contributions
  • For expanding the business, a different set of talents may be required
  • Let others step-in if these aren't your talents

Don't wait to get started

  • If you wait, you may have less flexibility in making trade-offs between the business and your standard of living
  • If you start early, your apatite for creature comforts and family responsibilities are not likely to be great, thus you'll be able to make different choices

Be a careful spender

  • Arrange your affairs such that on short notice you can afford to live without a salary for a year
  • Be willing to sacrifice personal comfort on behalf of your business

You are not your business

  • Your company's failures don't make you an awful person
  • Likewise, your company's successes don't make you a genius
